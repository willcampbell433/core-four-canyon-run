      Know Your Target

      What might eat the jig

      Bluefin tuna

      The freight train

      • ID: Dark steel-blue back, short pectoral fins, built like a torpedo.
      • Fight: Long deep runs. Settle in, keep pressure, let the harness work.
      • Eats: Trolled bars, jigs on the marks, chunked butterfish at night.
      • Table: Seared rare or straight sashimi. Ice it fast, bleed it faster.
      Yellowfin tuna

      The sprinter

      • ID: Bright yellow sickle fins and a gold stripe down the side.
      • Fight: Blistering first run, then stubborn circles under the boat.
      • Eats: Spreader bars, ballyhoo, poppers when they blitz.
      • Table: Sashimi grade. The canyon classic.
      Bigeye tuna

      The heavyweight

      • ID: Oversized eye, deeper body, longer pectorals than bluefin.
      • Fight: Pound for pound the meanest tuna in the canyon. Expect a war.
      • Eats: Best bite at dusk and dawn, loves a deep-dropped jig or trolled spread in low light.
      • Table: Fattier than yellowfin. Elite sashimi.
      Mahi mahi

      The acrobat

      • ID: Electric green and gold, blunt forehead on the bulls.
      • Fight: Jumps, flips, and never quits. Chaos on light tackle.
      • Eats: Anything near floating structure. Check every lobster pot ball and weedline.
      • Table: Fish tacos. Non-negotiable.
